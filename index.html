About me

I am a PhD student in statistics at the University of Victoria under the supervision of Dr. Laura Cowen. My MSc was in statistics at the University of New Brunswick under the supervision of Dr. Connie Stewart. My MSc research focused on developing methods of dietary estimation for marine predators. I am also an Associate Statistican (A.Stat.). Currently, I am a Co-Chair of the Student Committee of the Western North American Region (WNAR) of the International Biometric Society and the Student Representative on WNAR's Regional Advisory Board. I am also a member of the Student and Recent Graduates Committee of the Statistical Society of Canada (SSC). My research interests include: statistical ecology, methods for compositional data, capture-recapture models, statistical computing, dietary estimation, fatty acid signature analysis.

QFASA R package

The model I developed for my masters thesis in now included in version 1.1.1 of the R package QFASA.
Accurate estimates of the diets of predators are required in many areas of ecology, but for many species current methods are imprecise, limited to the last meal, and often biased. The diversity of fatty acids and their patterns in organisms, coupled with the narrow limitations on their biosynthesis, properties of digestion in monogastric animals, and the prevalence of large storage reservoirs of lipid in many predators, led to the development of quantitative fatty acid signature analysis (QFASA) to study predator diets. The package QFASA is available on CRAN: https://cran.r-project.org/package=QFASA
